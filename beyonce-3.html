Beyoncé aka Beyoncé Giselle Knowles-Carter is an American singer, actress, songwriter, record producer, dancer, director, philanthropist and entrepreneur. As of 2022, Beyoncé’s net worth is $500 million. She became the highest-earning Black musician in History, in the year 2014.

She is active in the industry since 1987. In the late 1990’s she was the lead singer of ‘‘Destiny’s Child’’, one of the best-selling girl groups of all time which rose her to fame. She is the world’s one of the best-selling recording artists who has sold more than 118 million records worldwide.

Beyoncé Wiki/Biography

Born on 4 September 1981, Beyoncé’s age is 41 Years Old as of 2022. She was born in a well-settled family from Houston, Texas, The United States. She was ranked the most powerful female in the entertainment industry in 2015 and 2017 by Forbes. She holds an American nationality and has her belief in Christian religion.

Career

Beyoncé started her career as a lead vocalist of the R&B group ‘’Destiny’s Child’’. She started her career alongside her childhood friend Kelly Rowland. They both were placed in a group with 3 other girls called ‘’Girl’s Tyme’. They did a talent show circuit in Houston dancing and rapping as well.

Arne Frager, the producer of R&B saw this and brought them to ‘Star Search’ which was the biggest talent show on national television of that time by Northern California Studio. However, they didn’t perform well and hence failed. The girls later auditioned and were selected for Elektra Records and then moving to Atlanta Records.

On 5 October 1995, the group was signed by Dwayne Wiggins’s Grass Root Entertainment. Later the group signed a contract with Columbia Records. In 1996, the group ‘’Girl’s Tyme’’ changed their name to ‘’Destiny’s Child’’ based upon a passage in the book Isaiah. In 1997 they released ‘Killing Time’ their major label debut song on the soundtrack of the major 1997 hit film ‘Men In black’.

Their 1st major hit ‘No, No, No’ was released in the November of the same year. In February 1998 they released their self-titled debut album which won them 3 major awards. In 1999, they released their Multi-Platinum 2nd album ‘The Writings on the Wall’. They also released ‘Bills, Bills, Bills’, ‘Jumpin, Jumpin’ and ‘Say My Name’. The song ‘Say My Name’ became their most successful song and their signature song as well and won them a couple of awards. 

In 1999, Beyoncé along with the original member of ‘Boyz II Men’, Mare Nelson released a duet titled ‘After All is Said and Done’ for the film ‘The Best Man’. For the 2000 film ‘’Charlie’s Angels’ the band recorded ‘Independent Women Part 1’, which was their best charting single. In early 2001, she starred in a major role in ‘Carmen: A Hip Hopera’, the MTV made-for-TV film. 

In May 2001, their 3rd album ‘Survivor’ was released which was a huge success but also Roberson and Lockett, the other former members of the group filed a lawsuit at them, This album debuted Number 1 on the US Billboard 200 with huge sales. The singles ‘Survivor’ and ‘Bootylicious’ also went great and won them a few awards including a Grammy Award.

In October 2001, they released their holiday album ‘8 Days of Christmas’ and later the group announced that they now are going to take a break from the group and are going to further pursue solo careers. Then in July 2002, Beyoncé made her theatrical film debut with the comedy film ‘Austin Powers in Goldmember’ which was a huge success. As a lead single, she released ‘Work It Out’ which entered the Top 10 in Norway, UK, and Belgium. 

In 2003, she starred in the musical comedy ‘The Fighting Temptations’, which was again a great success but with mixed critic reviews. She also contributed to the soundtrack ‘Summertime’. In October 2002, her 1st solo recording with the song ’03 Bonnie and Clyde’ by Jay-Z was released peaking No. 4 on the US Billboard Hot 100. 

On 14 June 2003, she had her 1st pay-per-view TV special ‘Ford Presents Beyoncé Knowles, Friends, and Family, Live’ from Ford’s 100th Anniversary Celebration, in Dearborn, Michigan, US. For this, she featured the songs from her 1st solo album ‘Dangerously In Love’. This was officially released on 24 June 2003 and topped Number 1 on the US Billboard 200 and was a worldwide success.

‘Crazy In Love’, the song from the album ‘Dangerously In Love’, the lead single featuring Jay-Z became her 1st Number 1 single as a solo artist in the US. Later her songs ‘Baby Doll’, ‘’Me, Myself and I’, and ‘Naughty Girl’ also topped the charts. The album won her 5 major awards. In November 2003, she went for ‘Dangerously In Love Tour’ in Europe and later in ‘Verizon Ladies First Tour’ in North America. 

On 1 February 2004, she was given the honor to perform the American National Anthem at Super Bowl XXXVIII, Reliant Stadium, Houston, Texas. On 15 November 2004, her album ‘Destiny Fulfilled’ was released which topped Number 2 on Billboard 200. This included the singles ‘Soldier’, ‘Lose My Breath’ which did great on charts. This also breaks a few records.

After the huge success of ‘Destiny Fulfilled’ she went for the Worldwide Concert Tour ‘’Destiny Fulfilled… and Lovin’ It’’, sponsored by McDonald’s corporation. On 25 October 2005, the group ‘’Destiny’s Child’’ got back in the game after the break and released their 1st compilation album ‘Number ones’. In March 2006, this album accepted a star on ‘The Hollywood Walk of Fame’ and was a huge commercial success.

On 4 September 2006, her 2nd solo album ‘’B’Day’’ was released coinciding with her 25th Birthday which debuted Number 1 on the Billboard 200 and was a huge commercial success as well. The album’s lead single ‘Déjà vu’ featured Jay-Z which also went great on charts. Her 2nd international single ‘Irreplaceable’ was a worldwide commercial success and reached Number 1 in 5 countries. 

There were 3 songs part of the album ‘’B’Day’’, which were ‘Get Me, Bodied’, ‘Ring the Alarm’, and ‘Green Light’, which were only released in the United Kingdom. ‘’B’Day’’ was nominated for 5 Grammy awards and many more awards as well. In the year 2006, she worked in the comedy film ‘The Pink Panther’ which did great on the Box Office Worldwide.

She also worked in the film ‘Dreamgirls’, which was again a huge success. To promote this film, she released the lead single ‘Listen’ from the soundtrack album. In April 2007, she made her 1st Worldwide Concert Tour ‘The Beyoncé Experience’ visiting 97 venues and grossed over a whopping $24 Million. Later, ‘’B’Day’’ was re-released with her debut ‘Beautiful Liar’ with Shakira and 4 other songs.

On 18 November 2008, her album ‘I Am… Sasha Fierce; was released which received mediocre reviews from the critics but debuted Number 1 on Billboard 200 and was a huge commercial success. This was also her 3rd consecutive Number 1 album in the US. This album included the Number 1 song ‘Single Ladies (Put a Ring on it)’ and the Top 5 songs ‘Halo’ and ‘If I Were a Boy’.

The album ‘I Am… Sasha Fierce’ also included the songs ‘Sweet Dreams’, ‘Video Phone’, ‘Diva’, ‘Broken-Hearted Girl’, and ‘Ego’ which all were successful. In 2009, she went for her 2nd headlining Worldwide Concert Tour ‘I Am World Tour’, which consisted of 108 shows and grossed a whopping amount of $119.5 Million.

In the 2008 musical biopic ‘Cadillac Records’ she started as the blues singer Etta James. Her performance here was highly appreciated by the critics and led her to a few awards as well. On 20 January 2009, at the couple Barack and Michelle Obama’s 1st inaugural ball, she performed ‘At Last’ by James. She later starred in the thriller ‘Obsessed’. The film received negative reviews but did well at the US Box office.

Beyoncé featured and appeared on the music video of ‘Telephone’ by Lady Gaga which was highly successful. On 26 January 2011, she became the 1st solo female artist in 20 years to headline the main Pyramid Stage at the 2011 Glastonbury Festival. Later her 4th solo album titled ‘4’ was released which again topped Number 1 on the Billboard 200.

The album ‘4’ was preceded by 2 singles ‘Best Thing I Never Had’ and ‘un the World (Girls)’. The 4th single ‘Love on Top’ went great on charts. Album ‘4’ also included other singles which were ‘Countdown’, ‘I Care’, ‘End of the Game’, and ‘Party’. She wrote a cover story for ‘Essence’ which detailed her 2010 career break titled ‘Eat, Play, Love’ which won her a writing award from the New York Association of Black Journalists.

In late 2011, at the New York’s Roseland Balloon, she took the stage for 4 nights of special performances called ‘The 4 Intimate Nights With Beyoncé’ performing her album ‘4’. On 1 August 2011, the album ‘4’ was certified Platinum by RIAA and broke multiple records. In June 2012, to celebrate the resort’s opening she performed 4 nights at the Revel Atlantic City’s Ovation Hall. In January 2013, ‘’Destiny’s Child’’ released ‘Love Songs’ and a newly recorded track ‘Nuclear’.

She also performed the American National Anthem singing with the then-president Barack Obama’s 2nd inauguration in Washington D.C. She also performed at the Super Bowl XLVII halftime show at the Mercedes-Benz Superdome in New Orleans, Louisiana, US. On 16 February 2013, her feature-length documentary film ‘Life is but a Dream’ was aired on HBO, co-directed by Beyoncé herself.

On 15 April 2013, she started the most successful tour of all time titled ‘The Mrs. Carter Show World Tour’ which ran into 132 dates and ended in March 2014. In May 2014, her cover of ‘Back To Black’ on the Great Gatsby soundtrack was released. On 24 May 2013, she voiced in the 3D CGT Animated film ‘Epic’ by 20th Century Fox as Queen Tara and also recorded an original song for it titled ‘Rise-Up’.

Controversy

In 2016, her activewear brand Ivy Park came under fire following an investigation. According to the reports, the women working in the Sri Lankan factories were very underpaid and had to work 60 hours a week in terrible conditions. This was criticized by many.

During Blue Ivy’s birth, according to media reports, the couple paid $1 Million to the hospital to block an entire floor in Manhattan’s Lenox Hill Hospital. According to reports and witnesses, the entry was denied, cameras were shut down and people suffered a lot. This was denied by both, the couple as well as the hospital association.

During Beyoncé’s performance at the Super Bowl Halftime Show, she was flanked by the dancers dressed in outfits inspired by the Black Panthers as part of the act, which was not appreciated by the Police Unions and political rights across the country. She later apologized for it.

The couple once decided to go vegan temporarily and that time Beyoncé was seen wearing a fur collar entering a vegan restaurant. The media bashed the incident all day.

Facts and Information

Beyoncé on her mother’s side is a descendant of Jean-Vincent d’Abbadie de Saint-Castin and the Acadian leader Joseph Broussard. Later in 2018, after some research, she found out that she is a descendant of a slaveowner.

Beyoncé got interested in singing when her dance instructor Darlette Johnson was humming a song that Beyoncé completed beautifully and was able to hit the high-pitched notes.

At the age of 7, in a school talent show, she sang ‘Imagine’ by John Lennon and beat 15-16-year-olds.

For 2 years she was the member of the chair at St. John’s United Methodist Church as a colorist.

Mathew was managing the group ‘’Destiny’s Child’ from the very beginning. Letoya Luckett and Roberson, the other members of the group didn’t like his work and became unhappy and hence were replaced by Farrah Franklin (who was dismissed later) and Michelle Williams. Public, media, and critics blamed Beyoncé for this and later her long-lasting boyfriend left her at this time. She went through severe depression for a couple of years. She later said her mom helped her fight it.

During the tour ‘The Beyoncé Experience’ she did food donation drives at 6 major stops with her paster at St. John’s and America’s Second Harvest. 

‘Halo’, a single by Beyoncé was the longest-running Hot 100 of her career and helped her attain more Top 10 singles on the list than any other woman during the 2000s.

According to the Toronto Star, Beyoncé’s music videos for ‘Single Ladies’ was spawning the ‘first major dance craze’ of the internet age. The video won several awards as well.

Beyoncé donated her salary from the film ‘Cadillac Records’ to an organization of rehabilitation centers for heroin addicts around the country called the Phoenix House.

In January 2010, she announced a hiatus from her music career taking her mother’s advice ‘to live life, to be inspired by things again’. The hiatus lasted for 9 months during which she visited different cities and did various ballet performances.
