Logic Net Worth: Career &amp; Lifestyle

Publish date: 2024-08-13

Rapper, producer, and two times Grammy awards nominee Sir Robert Bryson Hall II, known professionally as Logic, net worth is $14 million.

Logic has released six studio albums to date. He released the mixtape Young, Broke & Infamous in 2010, which was just the beginning of his career.

With the song series Young Sinatra, he gained massive popularity.

His mixtapes 2013 Young Sinatra: Welcome to Forever eventually helped him sign a deal with Def Jam Recordings.

Likewise, his studio albums, 2014 Under Pressure and 2015 The Incredible True Story, ranked within the top five on Billboard 200 in the US. The Incredible True Story was later certified platinum by RIAA.

Similarly, Logic’s 2017 album Everybody charted at number one in the United States, which gained his mainstream popularity.

It was certified platinum by RIAA while its lead single, 10800-273-8255, peaked at number three in the Billboard Hot 100 in the US and was a top-ten hit internationally.

Moreover, Logic has collaborated with numerous A-listers over the years, including Eminem, Wiz Khalifa, Wu-Tang, Khalid, and many more.

His other albums also achieved similar commercial success as his previous ones.

After releasing his sixth studio album No Pressure in 2020, Logic retired from the music industry. However, he returned in 2021.

Early Life

Logic was born Sir Robert Bryson Hall II on January the 22nd, 1990. He grew up with his seven half-siblings between his father, an African-American Maryland native, and a white mother.

Both of his parents were addicted to substance abuse father being addicted to Cocaine and his mother to alcohol.

He spent almost all his youth in the West Deer Park neighborhood, Maryland.

His brothers used to produce and distribute crack ad cocaine to addicts all over the block, including his father.

Therefore, Logic knows precisely how to produce crack cocaine with these experiences.

Even though Logic was unable to connect with his father in his childhood because of his father’s addiction problems, he later connected due to his escalating rap career.

He was expelled from Gaithersburg High School after he began skipping classes in the tenth grade.
