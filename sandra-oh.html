Sandra Oh Biography

Sandra Oh is a Canadian actress known for her role as Cristina Yang on the ABC medical drama series Grey’s Anatomy, from 2005-2014, a role that earned her a Golden Globe, two Screen Actors Guild awards, and five nominations for the Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Supporting Actress in a Drama Series.

Sandra Oh Grey’s Anatomy

In 2005, she appeared in more than several films, Including David Slad’s, Hard Candy, and the Independent anthology drama 3 Needles (2005), opposite Chloë Sevigny and Olympia Dukakis. She was also a cast in a Medical Series called Grey’s Anatomy.

For her role in Grey’s Anatomy the medical series she won both a 2005 Golden Globe Award for Best Supporting Actress in a Series and a 2006 Screen Actors Guild Award for Outstanding Performance by a Female Actor in a Drama Series.  She was the host of the 28th Genie Awards on March 3, 2008. Sandra Oh Killing Eve

She announced that she might be teaming up with Canadian director Ann Marie Fleming In October 2014, to collaborate on an animated characteristic film titled Window Horses. She also regarded it as a role in the comedy movie Tammy (2014), playing the spouse of Kathy Bates’ character. In 2015, she starred on the Refinery29 comedy web series Shitty Boyfriends. Oh began filming the comedy film, Catfight (2016), in New York City in December 2015.

Beginning in April 2018, Oh began a leading role in the BBC America spy thriller series Killing Eve, playing British intelligence agent Eve Polastri whose quarry is psychopathic assassin Villanelle played by Jodie Comer, with the women developing a mutual fascination. Upon reading the series script, Oh did not realize she was being considered for a lead, stating that she had been “brainwashed” by years of being cast as the leads’ best friends. In 2018, Oh became the first actress of Asian descent to be nominated for the Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Lead Actress in a Drama Series, for that role. She also won the Golden Globe Award for Best Actress – Television Series Drama, becoming the first woman of Asian descent to win two Golden Globe Awards. Oh won Outstanding Performance by a Female Actor in a Drama Series at the SAG Awards in 2019.
