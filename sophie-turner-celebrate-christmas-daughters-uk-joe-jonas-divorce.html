Sophie Turner Wins Right To Celebrate Christmas With Daughters In UK Amid Joe Jonas Divorce

Publish date: 2024-07-13

Highlights

  • Sophie Turner is spending the holidays in England with her daughters while Joe Jonas remains in the United States.
  • Court documents reveal that Joe flew the girls to the UK to meet Sophie on December 17, and they will stay with her until January 7.
  • Joe and Sophie had previously reached a temporary custody agreement over their daughters, but their divorce is still ongoing.

It was only a few months ago that Sophie Turner alleged her estranged husband Joe Jonas was preventing her from taking their daughters to the U.K. by withholding their passports.

But things have clearly changed between the exes (and hopefully for the better), as the Game of Thrones actress is reportedly spending the holidays in her native England with her two girls, while Joe remains in the United States with his family.

Joe Jonas Isn’t Spending The Holiday With His Daughters

According to court documents obtained by PEOPLE Magazine, Joe flew the girls overseas to meet their mother on December 17 so they could spend the rest of the holiday season with her. The agreement dictates that Sophie will return the girls to New York state on January 7th, where they’ll then spend time with their father.

Despite reaching out for comment, Joe and Sophie’s representatives had nothing to say to PEOPLE.

It was reported in October that Sophie and Joe had reached a temporary custody agreement over their 3 and 1-year old daughters, which included how they’d split the upcoming holidays, as they navigate their ongoing divorce.

Joe filed for divorce in Florida in September, though Sophie claims she was shocked by his decision. The musician originally withheld their daughter’s passports, saying that taking them abroad would violate the divorce proceedings in the tropical state.

Sophie Previously Accused Joe Of Kidnapping Their Kids

Sophie responded by filing a lawsuit, claiming Joe’s actions were tantamount to kidnapping and he had no right to keep their passports.

Joe responded by releasing a statement denying Sophie’s claims and explaining his position. “Joe is seeking shared parenting with the kids so that they are raised by both their mother and father, and is of course also okay with the kids being raised both in the US and the UK,” his lawyer said in a statement.

“This is an unfortunate legal disagreement about a marriage that is sadly ending,” the attorney went on. “When language like ‘abduction’ is used, it is misleading at best, and a serious abuse of the legal system at worst.”

Eventually, the exes spent several days in mediation in New York before agreeing to a temporary custody arrangement, which apparently gave Sophie the right to have the girls in the U.K. over the holidays.

Though their divorce is ongoing, Sophie has already publicly moved on. She’s been seen getting close to British aristocrat Peregrine "Perry" Pearson. A report in November says Joe is angered to see Sophie with someone new in such a high-profile fashion, especially given how much attention is surrounding their divorce.

“Joe is livid with the latest coming from Sophie, making him look like a horrible father,” a source told Daily Mail. “'It is disgusting in his eyes what she has chosen to do.”

“Joe thought they were moving in a forward direction with the divorce and finding a way to co-parent but now it is just a nasty mess, and the latest lawsuit threw any type of positivity all off course,” the insider added.

For now, Joe hasn’t been romantically linked to anyone new nor has he commented on reports that his daughters are abroad with Sophie for the holiday season.
