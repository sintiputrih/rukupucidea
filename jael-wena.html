The Voice Australia Contestant Jael Wena Wiki | Bio

16-years-old Jael Wena (born on December 22, 2005) is an Australian singer from Melbourne who appeared for auditions in the famous singing reality show called “The Voice Australia.” This is not the first time Jael participating in a singing competition because before this, she was with her family in “The Voice Generations.”

In 2022, Jael came for The Voice Australia’s blind auditions, and it did not take the judges much time to recognize her voice as they know her from the “The Voice Generations” (2021) when The Wena family was one of the finalists.

This time Jael chose Whitney’s huge’s epic song “How Will I Know,” and it would not be an overstatement to say that she performed fantastically. She managed every Note flawlessly, making all the judges stand up and clap, whether Keith Urban, Jessica Mauboy, or Rita Ora.

Jael Wena Birthday & Family

On December 22, 2005, Jael Wena (age 16 years) was born to her father, David Wena, and mother, Bek Wena, in Melbourne City in Australia. It is Jael’s mom Bek who taught her singing. Wena’s younger sisters, Esther and Shenae, are also singers, whereas her brother Israel is a musician and plays drums.

The Australian girl has been participating in different singing competitions since her childhood. Jael’s dad took her to participate in a competition organized by Fast Track Talent, which she won, beating all other contestants, which ignited a fire in her, after which she developed a competitive instinct. At present, she is attending school but focusing more on her singing.

Participation in The Voice Generations

In 2021, the Wena family entered the first season of The Voice Generations through blind auditions. Jael, Esther, Shenae, and their mom sang, whereas Israel and David played instruments.

They performed on Sister Sledge’s “We Are Family,” which they executed so beautifully that even Keith Urban got forced to give them a standing ovation. Unfortunately, after clearing auditions, the Wena family returned home because of COVID-19, but they were finalists and were on Guy Sebastian’s team.

Jael also got the opportunity to represent Australia in “Junior Eurovision,” where she got 3rd place but was able to provide her country with the best results. She also sang the National Anthem at the grand finale of A-League Soccer (2021).
