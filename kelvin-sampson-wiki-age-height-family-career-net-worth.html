Sampson was captain of his high school basketball team for two years, and played for his father John W. “Ned” Sampson.

Kelvin Sampson Career

After leaving Michigan State, Sampson moved to Montana Tech where he became assistant coach. After serving as an interim coach for one season, Sampson guided the Orediggers to a 73–45 record in his final four seasons. Montana Tech had won just 17 games combined in the three years before his arrival. Sampson turned in three consecutive 22-win seasons and claimed three Frontier Conference championships at Montana Tech. He led his teams to two NAIA District 12 title games and was named the league’s coach of the year in 1983 and 1985.
