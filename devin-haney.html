Devin Haney Biography

Devin Haney is an American Professional Boxer, who was born and brought up in San Francisco, California, United States of America. He is widely known for holding the WBC lightweight title since 2019.

He is also known for being ranked as the world’s fourth-best active lightweight by various magazines which include The Ring magazine, Transnational Boxing Rankings Board, and BoxRec, and fifth by ESPN.

In 2019, Devin became the youngest world champion in the last 23 years by becoming the World Boxing Council lightweight champion after competing in a final eliminator with Russias Zaur Abdullaev.  At the regional level, he has gone on to win the WBC Youth, IBF-USBA, IBF North American, WBC International, WBO Inter-Continental, and WBA International lightweight titles as a professional.

In April 2019, Haney Promotions signed a co-promotional deal with Matchroom Boxing and Eddie Hearn. In 2019 it was reported that Haney would fight Zaur Abdulaev for the WBC interim lightweight title and became the youngest world champion in boxing when he was elevated as the WBC lightweight champion.
