Steven Tyler

Steven Tyler, better known by his stage name, was born Steven Victor Tallarico on March 26, 1948, in Boston, Massachusetts. He is an American musician best known as the lead singer of the rock group Aerosmith, in which he also performs on harmonica, piano, and drums.

He is known to as the “Demon of Screamin'” because of his strong, broad vocal range and high-pitched screams. Additionally, he is highly known for his on-stage gymnastics. During his performances, Tyler often wears brightly colored (and occasionally androgynous) clothing, along with his recognizable scarves that dangle from his microphone stand.

Family

Steven wed Cyrinda Foxe, a former Warhol model who had been David Johansen’s ex-wife and the mother of model Mia Tyler, (born on December 22, 1978). Foxe and Tyler got divorced in 1987.

Tyler wed Teresa Barrick, a fashion designer, on May 28, 1988, in Tulsa, Oklahoma. He had two children with Barrick: Chelsea, a daughter, in 1989, and Taj, a son, in 1991. The pair announced their separation from one another in February 2005, citing personal issues. In January 2006, the divorce became final.

Children

Steven Tyler has 4 children including Mia Tyler in his first marriage with Foxe and Chelsea, Taj whom he had with Barrick, and also Liv Tyler whom he had with Bebe Buell.

Parents

Victor A. Tallarico (May 14, 1916 – September 10, 2011), a pianist and classical musician who taught music at Cardinal Spellman High School in the Bronx, and Susan Ray (née Blanche; June 2, 1925 – July 4, 2008), a secretary, are the parents of  Steven Tyler.
